Kings

The supreme rulers of medieval society, kings held absolute power over their kingdoms. They commanded armies, controlled vast territories, and were believed to rule by divine right. Medieval kings were responsible for maintaining order, dispensing justice, and protecting their realm from enemies. They lived in magnificent castles and were surrounded by elaborate courts filled with nobles, advisors, and servants.

Lords

Noble landowners who held territories granted by the king, lords were the backbone of the feudal system. They controlled vast estates, collected taxes from peasants, and provided military service to their king. Lords lived in fortified manor houses or castles and wielded considerable local power. They were responsible for maintaining law and order in their domains and protecting their vassals in times of war.

Knights

Elite warriors bound by the code of chivalry, knights were the professional soldiers of medieval society. They served their lords in exchange for land grants and were expected to be brave, honorable, and skilled in combat. Knights underwent rigorous training from childhood and were equipped with expensive armor and weapons. They participated in tournaments, crusades, and defended their lord's territories with unwavering loyalty.

Peasants

The majority of medieval society, peasants were farmers and laborers who worked the land. They lived in small villages and were bound to their lord's estate, unable to leave without permission. Peasants provided food and labor that sustained the entire feudal system. Despite their hard lives, they developed strong community bonds and maintained rich traditions through festivals, crafts, and oral storytelling.
